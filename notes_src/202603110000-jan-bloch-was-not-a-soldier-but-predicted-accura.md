Jan Bloch was not a soldier. He was a banker who was born into a poverty in Warsaw in 1836, but worked his way up to become a wealthy railroad financier in Russian controlled Poland. He was passionate about military issues and for years obsessively studied how the new tech technologies of his era would change warfare. He predicted a far more lethal battlefield than his contemporary could ever imagine. He discovered that the invention of smokeless gunpowder would literally lift the fog of war that hung over past complex and opposing armies would remain dangerously exposed.
