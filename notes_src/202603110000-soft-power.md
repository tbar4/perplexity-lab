As it has been formulated in international relations theory, soft power is the ability to influence the preference of other actors through perceived shared values, cultural attractiveness, and persuasive ideas. Whereas hard power depends on military strength and the ability to credibly threaten the use of force to achieve strategic goals, soft power draws on such disparate sources as technology, education, and commerce to extend a country’s influence.
